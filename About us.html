Overview of Sungai Buloh

An Overview

Sungai Buloh is a town located in the state of Selangor, Malaysia. It is situated approximately 20 kilometers northwest of the capital city, Kuala Lumpur. The town is known for its historical significance, as it was the site of the first leprosy settlement in Malaysia. Over the years, Sungai Buloh has evolved into a vibrant and diverse community with various amenities and attractions. The name “Sungai Buloh” means “River of Flowers” in Malay, referring to the nearby Sungai Buloh River. The town has experienced significant growth and development over the years, transforming from a small fishing village into a thriving residential and commercial area.

Sungai Buloh History

The Early History of Sungai Buloh

Sungai Buloh is a township located in the district of Gombak, Selangor, Malaysia. Its early history dates back to the pre-colonial era, when it was a small fishing village inhabited by the indigenous Orang Asli people. The area was known for its rich natural resources, including fertile lands and abundant fish in the Sungai Buloh River.

The British colonial administration began to develop the area in the late 19th century, establishing a police station and a post office in the 1890s. This marked the beginning of modernization and urbanization in Sungai Buloh, which attracted more settlers and businesses to the area.

In the early 20th century, the construction of the Sungai Buloh Dam was initiated to control floods and provide water supply for the growing population. This development project further transformed the landscape of the region and contributed to the growth of the township.

Development and Expansion

In the late 19th and early 20th centuries, the British colonial administration established rubber plantations in the area, which led to a rapid influx of immigrants from China and India. This marked the beginning of significant growth and development for Sungai Buloh. The construction of the North-South railway line in 1932 further boosted the town’s development, connecting it to other major towns and cities in Malaysia.

Economy and Infrastructure

The economy of Sungai Buloh is primarily driven by its diverse industrial and commercial sectors. Major industries in the area include manufacturing, agriculture, and construction. The town is also home to several educational institutions and healthcare facilities, which cater to the needs of the growing population. The local government has played an essential role in promoting economic growth and providing essential services to the residents.

Modern History of Sungai Buloh

History

The modern history of Sungai Buloh began in the 1960s, as the township experienced rapid urbanization and industrialization. The construction of the North Klang Straits Bypass in the 1970s further improved connectivity and attracted more industries and businesses to the area.

the 1990s, the Malaysian government designated Sungai Buloh as an industrial park, which led to the establishment of several factories and manufacturing plants in the area. This development spurred economic growth and created job opportunities for the local population.

In recent years, the township has undergone significant changes, with the construction of new housing estates, shopping malls, and recreational facilities. Sungai Buloh has also become an important educational hub, with several schools, colleges, and universities in the vicinity.
