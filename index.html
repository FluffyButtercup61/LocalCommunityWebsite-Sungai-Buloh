Sungai Buloh, located on the outskirts of Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, is a town steeped in history and unique features. Renowned for the Sungai Buloh Leprosy Settlement, established in the early 20th century, the town played a crucial role in global leprosy research and treatment. Preserving tradition amidst urbanization, Kampung Baru Sungai Buloh offers a glimpse into rural life. The Sungai Buloh Hospital and historic railway station stand as landmarks, reflecting the area's healthcare advancements and transportation history. The Sungai Buloh Leprosy Settlement Museum provides insights into leprosy treatment history, while the nearby Fort Lukut replica adds a broader historical dimension. Sungai Buloh encapsulates a vibrant blend of healthcare milestones, cultural heritage, and historical landmarks, embodying the diverse narrative of the region in a nutshell.
